⏰ Topic 1

Adverbs of Frequency

Indican con qué frecuencia ocurre una acción.

100% — Always
I always warm up before running.
~90% — Usually
She usually plays volleyball on Saturdays.
~70% — Often
They often go cycling in the mountains.
50% — Sometimes
He sometimes forgets his water bottle.
~10% — Hardly ever
We hardly ever play rugby at school.
0% — Never
I never skip training sessions.
Position Rule: Adverbs of frequency go BEFORE the main verb, but AFTER the verb to be.
✅ I always play tennis. / ✅ I am always tired.
⏰ Topic 1

Adverbs of Frequency: Negatives & Questions

❌ Negative Sentences

don't / doesn't + adverb
I don't always win matches.
She doesn't usually play on Sundays.
to be + not + adverb
He isn't always on time.
They aren't usually tired after practice.

❓ Questions

Do/Does + subject + adverb + verb?
Do you always warm up before playing?
Does she often practice yoga?
To be + subject + adverb?
Are you usually tired after matches?
Is he never late for practice?
Additional Adverbs:
occasionally (ocasionalmente) · frequently (frecuentemente) · rarely (raramente) · seldom (rara vez, formal)
📊 Topic 2

Comparatives

Se usan para comparar DOS cosas o personas.

📏 Short Adjectives (1 syllable)
Rule: adjective + -er + than
old → older than
tall → taller than
fast → faster than
⚠️ Spelling: big → bigger (double consonant) · happy → happier (y→i)
📚 Long Adjectives (2+ syllables)
Rule: more + adjective + than
expensive → more expensive than
intelligent → more intelligent than
beautiful → more beautiful than
💡 Adjectives ending in -y (happy, funny) → add -ier
Irregular Comparatives: good → better than · bad → worse than · far → farther/further than
📊 Topic 2

Comparatives: Complete Rules

Adjective TypeRuleExample
1 syllable add -er fast → faster
small → smaller
1 syllable (CVC) double final consonant + -er big → bigger
hot → hotter
thin → thinner
Ends in -y change y → i + -er happy → happier
funny → funnier
shy → shyer
2+ syllables more + adjective beautiful → more beautiful
interesting → more interesting
Ends in -le / -ow / -er can take -er OR more simple → simpler / more simple
narrow → narrower / more narrow
Irregular special forms good → better
bad → worse
far → farther / further
Common Mistakes: ❌ more taller (double comparative) · ✅ taller
❌ more good · ✅ better
🏆 Topic 3

Superlatives

Se usan para comparar UNO contra todo el grupo (el/la más...).

📏 Short Adjectives (1 syllable)
Rule: the + adjective + -est
old → the oldest
tall → the tallest
fast → the fastest
⚠️ Spelling: big → the biggest · happy → the happiest
📚 Long Adjectives (2+ syllables)
Rule: the most + adjective
expensive → the most expensive
intelligent → the most intelligent
beautiful → the most beautiful
Irregular Superlatives: good → the best · bad → the worst · far → the farthest / furthest
Examples: "He is the most intelligent student in the class."
"This is the best pizza I've ever eaten."

bad → the worst

⚠️ Topic 4

Irregular Adverb Forms

Algunos adverbios no siguen las reglas normales y tienen formas especiales.

AdverbComparativeSuperlativeExample
well better best She sings better than me.
He performed best of all.
badly worse worst I played worse than yesterday.
She danced worst in the group.
far farther / further farthest / furthest He ran farther than before.
She threw furthest of all.
little less least I sleep less than my brother.
He studies least in the class.
much more most She cares more than anyone.
He talks most of all.
Important Distinction: good (adjective) → well (adverb)
✅ She is a good singer. / ✅ She sings well.
❌ She sings good. (incorrecto)
🎯 Topic 5

Adverbs of Degree

Indican intensidad. Modifican adjetivos, verbos u otros adverbios.

🔺 Strong Intensifiers
extremely — extremadamente
This test is extremely difficult.
absolutely — absolutamente
That's absolutely perfect!
completely — completamente
I'm completely sure.
very — muy
My cat is very friendly.
🔽 Moderate / Weak Intensifiers
quite — bastante
The movie was quite interesting.
rather — bastante (con matiz negativo)
It's rather cold today.
a bit — un poco
I'm a bit tired.
a little — un poco
She is a little nervous.
Position: Adverbs of degree go BEFORE the word they modify:
very tall · extremely beautiful · quite interesting

🏷️ Topic 7

Question Tags

Se usan para confirmar información o pedir acuerdo.

✅ Positive Statement → Negative Tag
You like pizza, don't you?
She is your friend, isn't she?
They play football, don't they?
He can swim, can't he?
You have finished, haven't you?
❌ Negative Statement → Positive Tag
You don't have a pet, do you?
She isn't coming, is she?
They don't play rugby, do they?
He can't drive, can he?
You haven't eaten, have you?
The Rule: Positive statement → negative tag
Negative statement → positive tag

🏃 Topic 8

Adverbs of Manner

Describen cómo se realiza una acción. Responden a la pregunta "How?"

📝 Formation
Rule: adjective + -ly
quick → quickly
careful → carefully
beautiful → beautifully
bad → badly
⚠️ Spelling: happy → happily (y→i) · terrible → terribly (e→y)
📌 Position in the sentence
1. End of the sentence (most common)
She sings beautifully.
2. After the object
He drives the car carefully.
3. Before the main verb (emphasis)
She quietly left the room.
Examples: "He ran fast." (adverb) · "She spoke softly." · "They played well."

⚠️ Topic 8

Adverbs of Manner: Exceptions

Algunos adverbios no terminan en -ly o tienen formas especiales.

AdjectiveAdverbExceptionExample
good well Forma especial (no "goodly") She sings well. (❌ She sings good.)
fast fast Igual que adjetivo He drives fast. (no "fastly")
hard hard Igual que adjetivo She works hard. (no "hardly")
late late Igual que adjetivo He arrived late. (no "lately")
early early Igual que adjetivo I woke up early. (no "earlyly")
straight straight Igual que adjetivo Go straight ahead.
friendly in a friendly way No existe "friendlily" She smiled in a friendly way.
lonely in a lonely way No existe "lonelily" He looked in a lonely way.
Key Distinctions: hard (adverb: con esfuerzo) vs hardly (adverb: apenas)
✅ She works hard. · ❌ She works hardly.
✅ I can hardly see. (apenas puedo ver)

📌 Topic 8

Adverbs of Manner: Position in the Sentence

✅ Position 1: End of sentence
She sings beautifully.
He drove carefully.
They played well.
Most common position.
✅ Position 2: After the object
She speaks English fluently.
He opened the door slowly.
She signed the document quickly.
✅ Position 3: Before the verb
She quietly left the room.
He suddenly stopped.
They angrily shouted.
Adds emphasis.
⚠️ Position 4: After the verb (with to be)
She is always late.
He is never rude.
They were extremely tired.
Tip: Usually, adverbs of manner go at the end of the sentence or after the object.
For emphasis, they can go before the verb.
⚖️ Topic 8

Adverbs vs Adjectives

Es crucial distinguir entre adjetivos (describen sustantivos) y adverbios (describen verbos).

AdjectiveAdverbExample
quick quickly ✅ She is a quick runner.
✅ She runs quickly.
careful carefully ✅ He is a careful driver.
✅ He drives carefully.
beautiful beautifully ✅ She is a beautiful singer.
✅ She sings beautifully.
good well ✅ She is a good dancer.
✅ She dances well.
fast fast ✅ He is a fast worker.
✅ He works fast.
hard hard ✅ This is a hard question.
✅ She works hard.
Common Errors: ❌ She sings good. → ✅ She sings well.
❌ He drives careful. → ✅ He drives carefully.
❌ She is a beautifully singer. → ✅ She is a beautiful singer.

📌 Summary

Complete Summary

⏰ Adverbs of Frequency
always · usually · often · sometimes · hardly ever · never
Position: before verb · after to be
📊 Comparatives
adj + -er · more + adj · irregular (better, worse)
Compare TWO things
🏆 Superlatives
the + adj + -est · the most + adj · irregular (best, worst)
Compare ONE vs the group
🏃 Adverbs (Comparative/Superlative)
fast → faster → fastest · carefully → more carefully → most carefully
well → better → best · badly → worse → worst
🎯 Adverbs of Degree
extremely · very · quite · a bit · too · enough · etc.
Go BEFORE the word they modify
❓ Questions with Prepositions
What are you looking for?
Preposition at the END in everyday English
🏷️ Question Tags
Positive → negative tag · Negative → positive tag
Special: aren't I? · shall we? · will you?

adj + -ly (quick → quickly)
Exceptions: well, fast, hard, late, early
Position: usually at the end of the sentence
