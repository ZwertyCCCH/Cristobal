The First Detective Story in History!

📖 ABOUT THE AUTHOR

Edgar Allan Poe (1809-1849)

Master of Horror

Famous for dark, mysterious stories and poems like "The Raven" and "The Tell-Tale Heart."

Father of Detective Fiction

Created the first fictional detective: C. Auguste Dupin.

American Writer

Born in Boston, died in Baltimore under mysterious circumstances.

Published 1841

Appeared in Graham's Magazine in Philadelphia.

Did you know? Poe's detective Dupin inspired Arthur Conan Doyle to create Sherlock Holmes nearly 50 years later!
👥 CHARACTERS

Who is in the story?

🕵️ C. Auguste Dupin

The First Detective — brilliant, eccentric, poor but happy with books. Lives in Paris with the narrator.

📝 The Narrator (unnamed)

Dupin's friend and roommate. He admires Dupin's genius and tells the story.

⚓ The Sailor

Owner of the orangutan. Innocent but terrified. Confesses the truth.

👵 Madame L'Espanaye

Old woman. Killed brutally. Her head is almost cut off.

👩 Mademoiselle L'Espanaye

The daughter. Strangled and stuffed inside the chimney.

🦧 The Orangutan

A large, powerful ape from Borneo. The real killer — but not guilty by intention.

🧠 DUPIN'S GENIUS

How Dupin Reads Minds

"Most men have windows over their hearts; through these he could see into their souls."

The famous "Fruit-seller" example:

Thought chain Dupin followed:

1. Fruit-seller with apples → 2. Cobblestones → 3. Stereotomy (stone-cutting) → 4. Epicurus (Greek philosopher) → 5. Orion (stars) → 6. Chantilly (short actor)
Lesson: Dupin observes tiny details and connects them logically. This is called ratiocination — logical reasoning.
🔪 THE CRIME

The Murders on Rue Morgue

Newspaper Report (July 7, 1840):

Two women were found dead in their home. The daughter's body was stuffed inside the chimney. The mother's head was nearly cut off. The room was in chaos — furniture broken, blood everywhere.

💰 Money Left Behind

Gold and valuables were found in the room — not stolen. So robbery was NOT the motive.

🔒 Locked Room

The door was locked from inside. Windows were closed. No way in or out — or so it seemed.

Mystery: If the room was locked from inside, how did the killer escape?
🔍 DUPIN'S INVESTIGATION

What the Police Missed

🪟 The Windows

One window had a broken nail. The nail only looked like it held the window closed. The window had a hidden lock!

⚡ The Lightning Rod

A metal pole outside the building. Someone (or something) could climb it to reach the window.

💪 Inhuman Strength

The daughter's body was stuffed high up in the chimney. Only tremendous strength could do that.

🗣️ The Voices

One voice spoke French. The other voice — high and strange — no one could understand.

"What seemed to be not possible we have proved to be possible."
🦧 THE SOLUTION

The Killer is NOT Human!

The Clues:

• Hair found in the victim's hand — gray, coarse, NOT human hair.
• Handprint on daughter's neck — too large for any human hand.
• The voice no one could understand — not a human language.
• The wild, chaotic room — like an animal in a frenzy.
• The incredible strength — more than any human.
Conclusion: The killer was an ORANGUTAN — a large, powerful ape from Borneo.
⚓ THE CONFESSION

The Sailor's Story

"It was that animal! The orangutan!"

• The sailor caught the orangutan in Borneo.
• He brought it to Paris.
• The animal escaped, climbed the lightning rod, and entered the window.
• The sailor followed but could not stop the killings.
• He watched in horror as the animal killed both women.
• He ran away in fear.
The sailor is innocent! He did not commit the murders. The animal acted out of fear and confusion.
🏆 LITERARY IMPORTANCE

Why "The Murders in the Rue Morgue" Changed Literature

First Detective Story

Created the fictional detective genre. Dupin is the prototype for Sherlock Holmes, Hercule Poirot, and all others.

Locked-Room Mystery

Invented the "impossible crime" subgenre. A crime committed in a room locked from inside.

Ratiocination

Introduced logical reasoning as the method to solve mysteries. The detective uses observation and logic.

Inspired Many Writers

Arthur Conan Doyle (Sherlock Holmes), Agatha Christie, and countless others.

💡 THEMES

What Does the Story Teach Us?

Reason vs. Imagination

Dupin uses logic. The police use imagination and fail. True answers come from careful reasoning.

Appearance vs. Reality

Things are not always what they seem. The locked room had a hidden opening; the killer seemed human but was an animal.

Nature vs. Civilization

The orangutan represents wild, uncontrolled nature. It is an innocent killer — it didn't know it was wrong.

Justice vs. Injustice

The innocent sailor is terrified but not guilty. Dupin helps the truth come out.

⚔️ COMPARISON

Dupin vs. Sherlock Holmes

Auguste Dupin (1841)

  • First fictional detective
  • Lives in Paris
  • Unnamed narrator friend
  • Uses "ratiocination" (logic)
  • Solitary, eccentric

Sherlock Holmes (1887)

  • Most famous detective
  • Lives in London
  • Dr. Watson as narrator
  • Uses "deductive reasoning"
  • Brilliant, eccentric
Arthur Conan Doyle was directly inspired by Poe's Dupin when creating Sherlock Holmes!
✅ SUMMARY

What We Learned Today

  • Edgar Allan Poe wrote the first detective story in 1841.
  • C. Auguste Dupin is the first fictional detective — brilliant, logical, eccentric.
  • The murders were committed by an orangutan, not a human.
  • The killer escaped through a window that seemed locked, using a lightning rod.
  • Dupin solved the mystery using logic and observation — not guesses or imagination.
  • This story created the locked-room mystery genre.
  • It inspired Sherlock Holmes and all later detective fiction.
