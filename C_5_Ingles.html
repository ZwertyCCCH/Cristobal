📖 VOCABULARY

Friendship verb phrases

🤝 Positive relationships

get on = have a good relationship

get to know = learn about someone

hang out = spend time relaxing

meet up = see each other

keep in touch = stay connected

make friends = start new friendships

💔 Difficult moments

argue = disagree angrily

break up = end a friendship/relationship

lose touch = stop communicating

miss = feel sad when someone is absent

💡 Examples:
"I hang out with my best friend every weekend."
"We keep in touch on WhatsApp."
"I don't want to lose touch with my old friends."

📝 GRAMMAR PART 1

Questions with prepositions

Some verbs are followed by prepositions (look for, talk about, listen to). In questions, the preposition goes at the END.

✅ Correct

What are you looking for?

Who are you talking to?

What are you thinking about?

❌ Incorrect

For what are you looking?

To whom are you talking?

About what are you thinking?

⚡ Remember: In everyday English, we put the preposition at the end of the question.
🏷️ GRAMMAR PART 2

Question Tags

We use question tags to check information or ask for agreement.

Structure: Statement + short question (opposite polarity)

➜ Positive statement → negative tag
"You like pizza, don't you?"

➜ Negative statement → positive tag
"You don't have a pet, do you?"

Present Simple

She loves cats, doesn't she?

They play football, don't they?

Past Simple

You went to the party, didn't you?

She didn't call, did she?

🔍 GRAMMAR PRACTICE

Common preposition questions

Examples:

➜ What are you looking at? (see / focus on)
➜ What are you listening to? (music / podcast)
➜ What are you thinking about? (ideas / memories)
➜ What are you waiting for? (bus / friend / answer)
➜ Where are you hanging out? (park / mall / home)
📌 Question tags for predictions: "You live near here, don't you?"
"Your birthday is in July, isn't it?"
✍️ WRITING

Types of poems about friendship

Haiku

Japanese poem, 5-7-5 syllables. Simple and elegant.

Acrostic

First letters of each line spell a word (e.g., FRIEND).

Limerick

5 lines, funny, rhyme scheme AABBA.

Cinquain

5 lines, syllable pattern: 2-4-6-8-2.

🍃 HAIKU

5-7-5 syllables

Example:
Primary school (5)
Run, fall down, get up, smile, play (7)
I want this forever (5)
Another example:
Group of best friends (5)
Sitting around the beach fire (7)
Everyone's laughing (5)

💡 Topic idea: Write a haiku about a happy moment with friends.

😂 LIMERICK

Funny 5-line poem (AABBA)

I have a friend who loves the sun (A)
So outside we often have fun (A)
Too long outside! (B)
Go and hide! (B)
His face is redder than anyone! (A)

🎭 Rhyme scheme: Lines 1,2,5 rhyme with each other. Lines 3,4 rhyme together.

📝 CINQUAIN

Syllable pattern: 2-4-6-8-2

Look up ... (2)
Someone is there (4)
Waiting for you, happy (6)
Let's chat about the world all day (8)
With friends! (2)
💡 Tip: Cinquains don't need to rhyme. Focus on describing a moment or feeling.
🎙️ ORACY

Speaking with confidence

Eye contact

Making eye contact helps you connect with your audience. It shows you are paying attention.

Project your voice

Speak loudly enough so everyone can hear. Breathe from your stomach, not your chest.

Try this experiment:
1. Put your hand on your stomach
2. Breathe in for 5 seconds
3. Breathe out and say a line from your poem
4. Your voice will be stronger!
