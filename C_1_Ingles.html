🎯 Objetivo de la clase

Al finalizar la clase, los estudiantes podrán diferenciar y usar correctamente el present simple y el present continuous (incluyendo negaciones y preguntas), emplear adverbios de frecuencia y formar oraciones con gerundios, todo en el contexto de deportes y actividades físicas.

📘 Parte 1: Present Simple vs Present Continuous

🔹 Present Simple (Presente Simple)

📌 Uso:
  • Hábitos o rutinas
  • Hechos generales o verdades universales
  • Horarios fijos
📝 Estructura afirmativa: Sujeto + verbo (base + -s/-es para he/she/it) + complemento

✨ Ejemplos afirmativos (deportes):
  • I play football every Saturday.
  • She swims twice a week.
  • The match starts at 10 a.m.

❌ Present Simple - Negaciones

📝 Estructura negativa: Sujeto + do not (don't) / does not (doesn't) + verbo (base form) + complemento

✨ Ejemplos negativos (deportes):
  • I don't play tennis on Sundays.
  • She doesn't swim in winter.
  • He doesn't like losing matches.
  • We don't practice on Fridays.
💡 ¡Importante! Con he/she/it usamos doesn't y el verbo vuelve a su forma base (sin -s).

❓ Present Simple - Preguntas

📝 Estructura de preguntas: Do / Does + sujeto + verbo (base form) + complemento + ?

✨ Ejemplos de preguntas (deportes):
  • Do you play basketball every weekend?
  • Does she practice yoga in the morning?
  • Do they watch soccer on TV?
  • Does the match start at 3 p.m.?
💡 Respuestas cortas: Yes, I do / No, I don't   |   Yes, she does / No, she doesn't

🔹 Present Continuous (Presente Continuo)

📌 Uso:
  • Acciones que están ocurriendo ahora mismo
  • Situaciones temporales
  • Planes futuros cercanos (organizados)
📝 Estructura afirmativa: Sujeto + verbo to be (am/is/are) + verbo principal (-ing) + complemento

✨ Ejemplos afirmativos (deportes):
  • I am playing football right now.
  • She is swimming at the moment.
  • We are training for the competition this week.

❌ Present Continuous - Negaciones

📝 Estructura negativa: Sujeto + verbo to be (am/is/are) + not + verbo (-ing) + complemento

✨ Ejemplos negativos (deportes):
  • I am not playing football today.
  • She isn't swimming right now.
  • They aren't training this week.
  • He isn't wearing his helmet.
💡 Contracciones comunes: is not = isn't   |   are not = aren't   |   am not (sin contracción común)

❓ Present Continuous - Preguntas

📝 Estructura de preguntas: Verbo to be (Am/Is/Are) + sujeto + verbo (-ing) + complemento + ?

✨ Ejemplos de preguntas (deportes):
  • Are you playing basketball now?
  • Is she swimming at the moment?
  • Are they training for the competition?
  • Is the coach talking to the players?
💡 Respuestas cortas: Yes, I am / No, I'm not   |   Yes, she is / No, she isn't

🔸 Tabla comparativa: Afirmativo, Negativo, Pregunta

TenseAffirmativeNegativeQuestion
Present Simple He plays tennis. He doesn't play tennis. Does he play tennis?
Present Simple They run daily. They don't run daily. Do they run daily?
Present Continuous She is swimming now. She isn't swimming now. Is she swimming now?
Present Continuous We are training. We aren't training. Are we training?

⏰ Parte 2: Adverbs of Frequency (Adverbios de Frecuencia)

Uso: Indican con qué frecuencia ocurre una acción.
Regla: Van antes del verbo principal pero después del verbo to be.

Always
100%
I always warm up before running.
Usually
90%
She usually plays volleyball.
Often
70%
They often go cycling.
Sometimes
50%
He sometimes forgets his water bottle.
Hardly ever
10%
We hardly ever play rugby.
Never
0%
I never skip training.
📝 Ejemplos con el verbo to be:
  • I am always tired after a match.
  • He is never late for practice.
📝 Ejemplos en negaciones y preguntas:
  • I don't always win matches. (no siempre gano)
  • Do you always warm up before playing?
  • She isn't usually tired after practice.

🏃‍♂️ Parte 3: Gerunds (Gerundios)

¿Qué son? Un gerundio es un verbo que termina en -ing y funciona como sustantivo.

📌 Usos principales en deportes:

1. Como sujeto de la oración:
  • Swimming is good for your health.
  • Playing basketball requires skill.
2. Después de preposiciones:
  • Thank you for helping me with the warm-up.
  • He is interested in learning judo.
3. Después de ciertos verbos como: like, love, hate, enjoy, prefer, practice, finish, start, stop
  • I enjoy playing soccer with my friends.
  • She hates losing.
  • Let's practice kicking the ball.
💡 Gerundios en negaciones: I don't like losing matches. / She isn't interested in playing rugby.
